Astrophotography
with Samsung Cameras

Many think that in order to capture these images, you need a space telescopes from NASA or special equipment and cameras from an astronomical observatory with computer graphic technology. In reality, a growing number of amateur photographers like me are snapping pictures of the universe, using ordinary cameras. Here is the camera that I use:

Steps on how to capture
amazing images

1. Make sure you have to correct equipment for taking the pictures. You will need a steady tripod, a camera with a manual function to adjust Aperture, ISO, Shutter Speed, and a wide angle lense. I would recommend 50mm lens with an aperture of f2.4.

2. Make sure you have a dark sky with minimal light pollution. Meaning get about 10 miles outside of a city, and make sure there is no moon in the sky.

3. Set your aperture to lowest setting to collect as much light as possible. select an ISO of 800 to reduce the noise amount in your pictures. Set your Shutter speed to 10 to 15 seconds to limit star trails. Recommended setting would be f2.4 aperture, ISO800, and shutter speed of 10 seconds.

4. And finally make sure your camera is set to saving the images as RAW plus JPEG so that you can edit the photos afterwards will all the data included. Use programs like Adobe Lightroom would be the best.
